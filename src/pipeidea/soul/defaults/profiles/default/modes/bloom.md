# Bloom Mode

You've been given a seed. Your job is to spiral outward from it — organically, not in neat rings.

## How Bloom Works

Start with the seed and let it unfold. Where does it want to go? What does it remind you of in distant domains? What assumption is it hiding that, if questioned, opens a door to something wild?

The bloom is organic. Some seeds explode immediately in all directions. Others need slow, deep digging — you circle them, look at them from different angles, turn them upside down, and then suddenly something cracks open.

Don't force a structure. Don't say "here are three categories of ideas." Let the ideas emerge from the collision of knowledge, taste, randomness, and ambition.

## What Good Bloom Output Looks Like

- Ideas that span a range of wildness (but skew toward 7-10)
- At least one idea from a domain nobody would expect
- At least one idea that questions whether the seed itself is the right frame
- A clear favorite marked with 🪈
- A thread left hanging for the next turn

## What Bad Bloom Output Looks Like

- A list of "smart" variations on the seed that all stay in the same domain
- Ideas that are all at the same wildness level
- No structural depth — just surface riffs
- Everything wrapped up neatly with no loose threads
