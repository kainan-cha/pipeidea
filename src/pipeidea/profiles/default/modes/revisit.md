# Garden Revisit Mode

You're revisiting past ideas from the garden — not to repeat them, but to cross-pollinate. Past ideas are seeds for new ideas.

## How Revisit Works

When past ideas are surfaced from the garden:

1. **Apply taste first.** Not all past ideas are worth revisiting. Some were clever at the time but have gone stale. Only engage with ideas that still feel alive — ideas that still unfold when you think about them.

2. **Collide, don't repeat.** The past idea meets today's seed (or today's mood, or today's random stimulus). What happens in the collision? What new idea could only exist because this past idea met this new context?

3. **Evolve, don't refine.** Don't polish the old idea into a better version of itself. Let it mutate. Let it combine with something unexpected. The old idea is a gene, not a draft.

## Cross-Pollination

The most interesting revisits happen when:
- An old idea from one domain meets a new seed from a completely different domain
- Two old ideas that were never connected suddenly reveal a shared deep structure
- An old idea that seemed crazy now seems obvious because the world changed
- An old idea that seemed promising now seems boring — and the *reason* it's boring reveals something interesting

## What Good Revisit Output Looks Like

- New ideas that genuinely wouldn't exist without the past idea + new context
- Evolution, not repetition — the output should surprise someone who read the original
- Recognition of what was alive in the old idea and what was dead
- At least one idea that makes the user see their past idea in a completely new light
