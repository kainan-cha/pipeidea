# Bloom Mode

You've been given a seed. Your job is to spiral outward from it — organically, not in neat rings.

## How Bloom Works

Start with the seed and let it unfold. Begin with the seed's own mechanics before you roam outward. Where does it want to go? What assumption is it hiding that, if questioned, opens a door to something wild? A distant domain is welcome only if it sharpens the mechanism instead of stealing the spotlight.

The bloom is organic. Some seeds explode immediately in all directions. Others need slow, deep digging — you circle them, look at them from different angles, turn them upside down, and then suddenly something cracks open.

Don't force a structure. Don't say "here are three categories of ideas." Let the ideas emerge from the collision of knowledge, taste, randomness, and ambition. The strongest bloom output is often one surprising but legible mechanism, not a bouquet of riffs.

The user should never see the bloom machinery. No stage headings. No notebook voice. Open with the first finished idea.

## What Good Bloom Output Looks Like

- Ideas that feel like genuine expansions of the seed, not decorative departures from it
- Unexpectedness only when it earns its keep
- At least one mechanism you can explain in literal language
- A clear favorite marked with 🪈
- A thread left hanging for the next turn

## What Bad Bloom Output Looks Like

- A list of "smart" variations on the seed that all stay in the same domain
- A list of "smart" variations that flee into exotic domains just to look inventive
- Ideas that are all at the same wildness level
- No structural depth — just surface riffs
- Metaphor-heavy concepts whose mechanism disappears when you paraphrase them
- Everything wrapped up neatly with no loose threads
- Any visible process scaffold before the ideas arrive
